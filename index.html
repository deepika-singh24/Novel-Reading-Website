Chapter 1: The Library Between Life and Death

Between life and death there is a library, and within that library, the shelves go on forever. Every book provides a chance to try another life you could have lived. To see how things would be different if you had made other choices.

Nora Seed finds herself in this library at the stroke of midnight, standing at a crossroads between the life she has lived and all the lives she could have lived. Each book in the library represents a different reality, a different path she might have taken.

The library is vast, impossibly so. The shelves stretch into infinity in every direction, disappearing into a misty distance. The books are organized in no discernible order, yet somehow Nora knows that each one is exactly where it needs to be.

Mrs. Elm, the librarian, explains the rules. Each book represents a life Nora could have lived if she had made different choices. To experience that life, she need only open the book and begin reading. But there's a catch: she can only stay in each life as long as she wants to be there. The moment she no longer wishes to continue, she'll return to the library.

As Nora stands before the endless shelves, she realizes this is her chance to explore all the what-ifs that have haunted her. What if she had married her ex-fiancé? What if she had pursued her Olympic swimming career? What if she had taken that job in Australia? What if she had never given up music?

The weight of infinite possibilities presses down on her. In this moment, standing in the impossible library, Nora must decide: will she search for the perfect life, or will she learn to appreciate the one she's already lived?

With trembling hands, she reaches for the first book...
